What is Dungeons & Dragons?

D&D is a cooperative storytelling game where you and your friends go on adventures together — battling monsters, finding treasure, and making choices that shape the story. One player is the Dungeon Master (DM) who narrates the world. Everyone else plays a unique character.

🎲

Roll Dice

Dice decide outcomes. Want to pick a lock? Roll a d20 (a 20-sided die). High numbers = success. Low numbers = failure. It's thrilling every time.

📖

Tell a Story

The Dungeon Master describes the world. Your choices shape it. Will you sneak past the guards or challenge them to a fight? The story goes wherever you take it.

⚔️

Fight & Survive

When combat starts, turns are taken in order. Attack enemies, cast spells, use abilities. Work together — some enemies can't be beaten alone.

🗝️ Key Terms to Know

  • d20 — A 20-sided die. The most important die in D&D. Used for almost every action.
  • Ability Score — Six stats (Strength, Dexterity, etc.) that define what your character is good at.
  • HP (Hit Points) — Your health. Reach 0 HP and you fall unconscious.
  • AC (Armor Class) — How hard you are to hit. Attackers must roll over your AC to deal damage.
  • Initiative — A d20 roll at the start of combat to decide who goes first.
  • Dungeon Master (DM) — The player who runs the world, controls enemies, and narrates the story.
  • Party — Your group of adventurers. Teamwork is everything.
  • Saving Throw — A roll to resist danger (like dodging a fireball or resisting poison).

How to Play — Step by Step

D&D has a rhythm. Once you understand the loop, everything clicks. Here's how a session flows:

Before the game starts, you build your character using a Character Sheet. This records everything about who you are:

  • Choose a Race — Human, Elf, Dwarf, Halfling, etc. Each gives different bonuses.
  • Choose a Class — See the Characters section above.
  • Roll Ability Scores — Roll 4d6, drop the lowest, repeat 6 times. Assign scores to: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma.
  • Pick a Background — Soldier, Criminal, Sage? This gives you skills and a backstory.
  • Name your character — Make it yours.

💡 Tip: Use D&D Beyond (dndbeyond.com) for a free digital character sheet that does the math for you.

The DM describes where you are and what's happening. Example:

"You stand at the edge of a dark forest. The smell of smoke drifts through the trees. In the distance, you hear the crackle of fire and terrified screaming."

Now it's the players' turn to decide what to do. Talk to each other. Make a plan. The DM responds to whatever you choose.

Whenever you try something risky, the DM asks you to roll a d20 and add a relevant stat modifier.

Example: "I want to climb the wall." DM says: "Roll Strength (Athletics)." You roll a 12 + your Strength modifier of +3 = 15. The DM had a Difficulty Class (DC) of 13 in mind — you succeed!

🎲 d20 roll (12) + Ability Modifier (+3) = 15 vs DC 13 → ✅ Success!

When a fight breaks out, time becomes structured into rounds. Each round, every creature gets a turn in order of Initiative.

  1. Roll Initiative — Everyone rolls d20 + Dexterity modifier. Highest goes first.
  2. Your Turn: On your turn you get a Move (move up to your speed), an Action (attack, cast spell, help), and sometimes a Bonus Action.
  3. Attack Roll — Roll d20 + Attack Bonus. If the total beats the enemy's AC, you hit!
  4. Damage Roll — Roll your weapon's damage dice (e.g. a sword = 1d8 + Strength modifier).
  5. Repeat until one side is defeated, flees, or surrenders.

When your HP hits 0, you fall unconscious — but you're not dead yet! On each of your turns, roll a d20 with no modifiers:

  • 10 or higher = 1 success (need 3 successes to stabilize)
  • 9 or lower = 1 failure (3 failures = death)
  • Roll a 1 = counts as 2 failures
  • Roll a 20 = you regain 1 HP and wake up!

An ally can use their action to stabilize you with a Medicine check (DC 10) or a healing spell — saving you from rolling.

After adventures, characters rest and grow stronger.

  • Short Rest (1 hour) — Roll Hit Dice to recover some HP. Warlocks regain spell slots.
  • Long Rest (8 hours) — Fully restore HP and all spell slots. Do this between major encounters.
  • Level Up — After enough XP (experience points) from defeating enemies and completing goals, your character gains a level, improving stats and unlocking new abilities.
